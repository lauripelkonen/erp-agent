I get this question at least twice a week.

Short answer: You could ask ChatGPT to drive your car, but you probably shouldn't.

Here's the difference between general AI and specialized business automation:

**ChatGPT knows everything about nothing specific.**
It can chat about procurement best practices but can't connect to your supplier database.

**Business AI knows everything about something specific.**
It understands your exact pricing rules, supplier relationships, and approval workflows.

**Context matters exponentially in business systems.**

When ChatGPT processes "create purchase order for SKU 12345," it has no idea about:
- Your supplier tiers and pricing agreements
- Inventory levels across locations  
- Seasonal demand patterns
- Budget approvals and spending limits
- Quality issues with specific suppliers

A specialized ERP agent knows all of this.

It's the difference between asking a random person for directions vs. using GPS that knows traffic, construction, and your destination.

Both can help. Only one gets you there efficiently.

This is why the future of business AI isn't one giant model that does everything.

It's specialized agents that do specific things brilliantly.

What's your take - generalist AI or specialist automation for business?
